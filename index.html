Devices

I spend a lot of my time building eReaders.
In 2010 I started working on Kobo's first E-Ink device.
Since then, I've helped create 8 eReaders which got faster and prettier while winning industry awards.

The Kobo
"compact, lightweight, and affordable"
CNET

The original Kobo had a big blue button, 100 free books and would sync via Bluetooth.
This was my real life experience working with an ARM device, running Linux and writting C++.
I'm not going to lie - there was crying. Because of C++. Not Linux. Linux is awesome.
Most of my work involved writting the persistance layer and learning how to build UIs with Qt.

Kobo Wi-Fi
"Lightweight with a smart interface and store"
PC Pro

Not 6 months after the first one, the wifi version came out.
Crying happened less. But so did sleep.
It turns out building a store and synchronizing libraries over Wi-Fi can be hard. Who knew.

Kobo Touch
"Our surprise winner is the most natural e-ink reader we've ever used"
Wired.com

I love this device. Nice screen. Wi-Fi. Touch interface. Awesome.
All the pain leading up to this was totally worth it.
Also, you could now track your reading stats.... For the more competitve among us.

Kobo Glo
"One of the fastest, most responsive e-readers we've used."
PC World

As good as the Touch, but now you could read at night.
I was excited to retire several of my lamps, thanks to this device. It was a dark time for IKEA.
I spent a bunch of time building a custom caching layer to improve performance.

Kobo Mini
"The Mini is probably lighter than this week's issue of The New Yorker."
Huffington Post

This tiny device can fit anywhere.
In fact, I'm pretty sure, if I shook out my couch at least 3 would fall out.
Making sure a device this size is still usable, definitely had its challenges.

Kobo Aura HD
"the Porsche of eReaders."
PCMag.com

Little known fact.
All the extra screens saved from the mini were glued together to make this...
When I wasn't supergluing, I was helping to modernize the user interface.
Okay. And reading comics. For testing purposes of course....

Kobo Aura
"Kobo Aura is the best flagship eReader on the market."
Sept 5, 2013 Engadget

This is our souped-up Glo.
Better screen, better touch technology, way faster. Just all around, awesomer. Also, multi-touch (whoo!)
Much of my work was making books open faster and improving wireless reliability.

Kobo H2O
"the Kobo Aura H2O looks like a damp fine e-reader, both in the water and out."
Aug 27, 2014 Yahoo

The H2O is the first waterproof eink reader.
Which is really cool, because some people love reading in the bathtub.... Err... or so I hear...
Now, dealing with water can be kind of a pain. But it also has its upside. For instance you can go around calling yourself a "wet-mode expert". So that kind of evens out...

E-Ink readers are devices designed to mimic the experience of reading on a regular piece of paper. They can store most book libraries, have high resolution, low-glare screens, and the batteries can last for months.

Android Apps

I started playing around with Android in spring 2008 and have been releasing apps periodically since then.

Thanks Douglas Crockford!

Desktop Apps

My first project at Kobo, was building the original content processing system. Because it turns out we wanted to have books! It took books from publishers in various formats, parsed and converted them into a proprietary format to be consumed by our clients.

  • .NET
  • Windows
  • MS SQL

While making eReaders, we realized we'd need a desktop application. So we built one. You can browse the store, buy and read books with it. It also talks to our devices when they're plugged in to USB.

  • C++
  • Qt
  • Windows/Linux/OS X
  • SQLite
